What it does

Signature management, minus the price tag

The essentials the big tools charge per user for — done with files you host yourself.

Design it your way

A real visual editor — fonts, colours, links, images, layout. No rigid template; every signature is the user's own.

Multiple saved signatures

Keep a set — client-facing, internal, short reply — and switch between them. Rename and delete in a click.

Lands in the right place

One click drops your signature in after your new message and before the quoted thread — not buried at the very bottom.

Won't send without one

A send-time check stops an email going out with no signature — with a clear, deliberate override when you truly need it.

Private by design

Each person's signatures live in their own Microsoft 365 mailbox. No third-party server, no central database, nothing leaves your tenant.

Free, full stop

No per-seat licence, no trial clock. The files are hosted on free static hosting; you own and control all of it.

Built for compliance

Every signature, on the record

If your industry needs license numbers, regulatory disclosures and disclaimers on every message, Signify makes that the default — not an afterthought.

  • Put license numbers & disclaimers in, formatted your way
  • A send-check so unsigned email doesn't slip out
  • Per-person — everyone carries their own required text
Setup

Installed by your admin in minutes

Signify is deployed once, centrally, from the Microsoft 365 admin centre — then it just appears for your team.

1

Open Integrated Apps

In admin.microsoft.com → Settings → Integrated Apps → Upload custom apps.

2

Point it at the manifest

Choose “Provide link to manifest file” and paste the link you were given (or the one below), then Validate.

3

Assign your users

Pick the people or groups who should have Signify, review, and Finish.

4

It appears in Outlook

Within a few hours, Signify shows up in the Apps area of the Outlook compose window for those users.

Manifest link: https://manfredmaxx.github.io/EmailSignatures/manifest.xml
Signify uses Microsoft's event-based add-in feature for its send-check, so it must be deployed by an admin (centralised deployment) — it isn't a per-person self-install. A full step-by-step guide ships with the add-in.
